Faking GP Signs

01

Sharpies & 15-18s

Traditionally, GP signs are hand-drawn by the 15-18s every Friday after practice. As the team grow, this became a 4 hour commitment. This become impossible to continue, so I built a program to do it for us.

02

The Design

Every swimmer gets a unique sign. The generator selects color pairings and border frames, creating thousands of distinct style variations.

Fonts, Textures & Variations

The rendering system uses a custom digital design kit created specifically for the Great Piranhas team to simulate authentic GP signs.

Font 1

Marker Sans

A thick, uppercase font used for swimmer names, simulating drawing with a wide chisel-tip marker.

Font 2

Marker Sans Mini

A lighter, character-rich font containing lowercase rhyming couplets, and housing the vector icons mapped directly to characters 0-9.

Texture

Marker Pattern

To simulate real markers, I hand-scanned and edited a real marker drawing texture. This texture masks overlays this texture over text to mimic the hand-drawn feel.

Combinatorial Variations

By combining outer/inner border layers and shifting hues (their colors) dynamically, the mathematical layout possibilities are immense:

Borders

19.8 Million Border Configurations

To guarantee every swimmer receives a completely unique layout, the frame borders are built as two modular, overlaying parts:

Outer Frames (17 variations)

Four-sided border frames containing hand-sketched stripes, waves, and hashes.

Inner Frames (9 variations)

Interior border borders (including the signature gp border) that outline name boxes.

Independent Hue Rotations

An SVG feColorMatrix hue rotation filter rotates the color spectrum of each layer dynamically (0° to 360°).

17 (Outer) × 9 (Inner) × 360 (Outer Hues) × 360 (Inner Hues) = 19,828,800 Unique Frame Style Combinations!

Mascots & Stroke Icons

I embedded graphics directly into characters 0-9 of the custom Marker Sans Mini font. This keeps the document size tiny and ensures icons scale to any size without blurriness.
